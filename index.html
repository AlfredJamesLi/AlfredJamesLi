Hi there! I am currently a joint Ph.D. student in Data Science at the School of Data Science, City University of Hong Kong (Supervisor: Professor Xiangyu Zhao), and in Human Resource Management at the School of Labor and Human Resources, Renmin University of China (Supervisor: Professor Wenxia Zhou, Distinguished Professor and Second-rank Professor— the highest academic title in humanities and social sciences in mainland China). I am also affiliated with the Applied Machine Learning Lab (AML) at CityU. Before pursuing my Ph.D., I obtained a B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics from Beijing Normal University (Zhuhai Campus) and an M.A. in Applied Psychology from City University of Macao.

Research Interests

  • Structural equation modeling (SEM) of employment-related competencies, career success and AI-era outcomes among university students (mainly undergraduates) and research-track master/PhD students, based on demographic and survey data (N≈400–600) as baseline models.
  • Machine-learning and NLP-based text mining of recruitment texts and open-ended questionnaire responses to extract competence elements, build predictive models (classification and regression), and design human-like data augmentation pipelines.
  • Career and job recommendation systems: leveraging survey, behavioral and labor-market data to fine-tune models and develop career recommendation / counseling agents for students.
  • More broadly, NLP, large language models (LLMs) and big-data methods for labor-market analytics and social science research.
